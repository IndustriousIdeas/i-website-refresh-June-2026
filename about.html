About

Making the complicated usable.

For 20 years I’ve done one thing across very different rooms: take something genuinely advanced and make it accessible without dumbing it down.

I’m Katy Romero, a senior marketing executive with more than 20 years building brand and marketing for some of the world’s most trusted names: Google, Nest, One Medical, Peloton, Pinterest, and tonies. Industrious Ideas is where I bring that work to founders, growth-stage teams, and the people who have to live with the results.

The through-line has never changed. Each of those companies was a beloved consumer brand with a real revenue engine beneath it, and my job was to hold the story while building the engine. I build marketing engines: teams that deliver revenue, and messaging that makes complex ideas land in human language. That second part has been my edge.

Why AI, and why now

AI is the newest version of the same complexity I’ve spent two decades translating. It’s moving fast, the hype is loud, and most teams are being told to just go figure it out. That’s the gap I close. I build with these tools every day, including my own AI platform, so the advice comes from an operator who ships, not a slide.

My belief about how this works is simple: people on judgment, agents on production. Not fewer people. Better-aimed people, with a governed system doing the volume, and the brand voice and guardrails built in so the work scales without ever embarrassing the brand.
